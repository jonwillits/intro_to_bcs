### Introduction to Brain and Cognitive Science
This is a set of modules for “Introduction to Brain and Cognitive Science”. In this course, we study the human mind and brain through the lens of tracing its evolutionary development. Each module shows how a step in evolution added a capacity that solved problems for the organism in its environment. The course also integrates discussion of AI throughout the the course, talking about how discoveries and capabilities in AI have often paralleled these developments in evolution. 

In a more traditional cognitive science class, the course is more “interdisciplinary”, but the approaches of the different disciplines are quite segregated. You have a “philosophy” week and an “AI” week, and a “linguistics” week, and several psychology and neuroscience weeks that completely ignore the other disciplines. The goal here is to work towards a more integrative approach that still uses ideas and methods from the many disciplines, but weaves them together.

Organizationally, the first three modules (0,1, and 2) set the stage for what the course will cover and how. After that, most of these modules are organized around a three level structure. The first sub-module covers the computational level perspective on the topic, talking about the functional problem(s) the organism faced and how the new developments solved that problem. The second submodule covers the algorithmic/data structure level, talking about the topic at something like the cognitive psychology level, discussing the representations and processes that are needed to perform the function. The third submodule discusses the biological (or artificial) implementation of these algorithms and data structures. The lists below outline the general topic of each module's lectures.
